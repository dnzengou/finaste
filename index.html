ABOUT NIRDA

The National Industrial Research and Development Agency (NIRDA), is a government institution that has been mandated with a mission to enable a generation of industrial innovators to become competitive through technology monitoring, acquisition, development and transfer & applied research.

THE 2018 OPEN CALLS: BANANA WINE AND GARMENTS SECTION

The main objective of the NIRDA Open calls is to introduce firm-level innovations, which improve the ability and capacity of Rwandan enterprises to compete in strategic national and international markets, leading to increased profits, exports and the creation of decent and productive employment. NIRDA is hereby issuing an open calls campaign to all industries in Rwanda, engaged in the Banana Wine and Garments value chains, to apply for support geared towards increasing their competitiveness and increase in domestic production and exports.

BANANA WINE INDUSTRY

Who Can Apply

Applicants for NIRDA support must be established industries or enterprises within the Banana Wine Value Chain. Applications from new, emerging or start-ups are not acceptable at this stage.

Application Criteria

    In order to be eligible for support, applicants must:
  • Be a private sector enterprise or cooperative registered in Rwanda
  • Be directly responsible for the preparation and management of the project with their partners (i.e., not acting as an intermediary);
  • Have stable and sufficient financial resources to ensure the continuity of their organisation throughout the project and, if necessary, to play a part in financing it
  • Be able to demonstrate their experience and capacity to manage activities corresponding in scale and complexity with those defined in the project.

About the garment industry

  1. The industry directly employs approximately 25,000 Rwandans and indirectly employs a further 50,000 people
  2. The approximate requirement of clothing production for the Rwandan population is around 50,000,000 garments yearly. At this rate daily production should be 151,000 units per day. To produce this number, 10,000 industrial sewing machines are required and at the moment there are only 4,000 industrial sewing machines in Rwanda
  3. There is a great opportunity for NIRDA to directly impact and improve this industry
  4. Currently the Made in Rwanda programme is creating between 20,000 and 21,000 of jobs per year
  5. The industry sector has employed over 1,500 employees and intends to employ over 4,000 people

About the banana wine industry

  1. In 2015 the agriculture sector contributed 31% of Rwanda’s GDP (www.statistics.gov.rw)
  2. Bananas are the second most important crop in Rwanda, second only to beans (NISR SAS 2016)
  3. The predominant value added product made from bananas in Rwanda is the production of alcoholic beverages commonly known as Urwagwa (NIRDA Report 2016). Other products include banana wine, juice, spirit, chips and flour.
  4. The vast majority of beverages produced from bananas (banana wine, banana beer and related fruit alcoholic beverages) are produced by 45.2% of all SMEs in Rwanda. Over 29 banana varieties are grown for banana brewing (Ministry of Trade and Industry Horticulture Development and Promotion Strategy 2014-2019).
  5. The vast majority of enterprises (70%) still use manual processes with only 1% using automated processes for banana beer and wine production
